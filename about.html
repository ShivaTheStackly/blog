More About The Chip Shortage

Worldwide semiconductor supply issues began in 2020 and continue to slow vehicle production. Foundries are running at maximum capacity, yet demand for advanced driver‑assistance systems, EV power modules and infotainment chips keeps climbing. Analysts expect supply to stay tight through 2025 as new fabs come online and automakers secure long‑term wafer contracts.

Manufacturers are re‑engineering control units to reduce the total chip count, prioritising high‑margin models and exploring software‑defined architectures to mitigate future shocks.
